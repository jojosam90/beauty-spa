8 Medical-Grade Technologies

Each technology in our clinic has been selected based on clinical evidence, safety profile, versatility, and its ability to deliver visible results for Singapore skin tones.

01
Hair Removal

Soprano ICE Platinum

The global gold standard in permanent laser hair removal. Soprano ICE Platinum combines three laser wavelengths simultaneously — 755 nm Alexandrite, 810 nm Diode, and 1064 nm Nd:YAG — to target hair follicles at multiple depths and across all skin types. The integrated ICE cooling system maintains the skin surface at approximately 5°C throughout treatment, delivering a uniquely comfortable experience. FDA cleared. Clinically proven for all Fitzpatrick skin types I–VI, including the darker Asian skin tones common in Singapore. Achieves 90%+ permanent hair reduction over a standard course of 6–8 sessions.

02
Lifting & Tightening

HIFU — High Intensity Focused Ultrasound

The most scientifically validated non-surgical face and body lifting technology available. HIFU delivers precisely focused ultrasound energy to the SMAS (Superficial Muscular Aponeurotic System) layer — the same anatomical layer addressed in surgical facelifts — at depths of 1.5 mm, 3.0 mm, and 4.5 mm below the skin surface. This triggers controlled thermal coagulation points that stimulate a powerful, sustained collagen remodelling response. Results continue to develop for 3–6 months post-treatment and typically last 12–18 months. Effective for face lifting, neck tightening, brow lifting, jowl reduction, and body skin firming. No downtime, no incisions.

03
Pigmentation & Brightening

Q-Switch Nd:YAG Laser

A laser platform with two clinically proven wavelengths — 1064 nm and 532 nm — that selectively target melanin pigment deposits while preserving surrounding tissue. The Q-Switch delivery mode fires nanosecond pulses of energy, creating a photoacoustic effect that shatters melanin granules without thermally damaging the overlying skin. Effective for superficial and mid-level pigmentation, sun spots, age spots, melasma management, tattoo removal, and overall skin brightening. Its 1064 nm wavelength is particularly suited to deeper Asian skin tones as it bypasses epidermal melanin. Precise wavelength control enables targeting of both brown and red pigmentation.

04
Tightening & Contouring

RF — Radiofrequency

Radiofrequency energy penetrates the dermis and subcutaneous layer, generating precise, controlled heat that triggers immediate collagen fibre contraction and sustained new collagen synthesis. This makes RF one of the most versatile aesthetic technologies available — effective for facial skin tightening, pore minimisation, body contouring, cellulite treatment, and overall skin quality improvement. RF is safe for all skin types and skin tones, as the energy targets tissue resistance rather than melanin — making it completely colour-blind and free from any risk of pigmentary side effects. Monopolar, bipolar, and fractional RF variants in our clinic allow us to precisely control depth and intensity for each treatment application.

05
Pigmentation & Scars

Pico Laser

Pico Laser delivers ultra-fast picosecond (one trillionth of a second) pulses of laser energy — approximately 100 times faster than nanosecond Q-Switch devices. This speed creates a predominantly photomechanical (rather than photothermal) effect, shattering pigment particles with tremendous precision while generating virtually no heat in surrounding tissue. The result: exceptional efficacy for stubborn pigmentation, deep hyperpigmentation, melasma, acne scars, enlarged pores, and overall skin texture improvement — with minimal to no downtime in most cases. Pico Laser's FOCUS lens array also generates microscopic treatment zones that trigger collagen induction without ablating the skin surface — making it uniquely effective for textural and scar remodelling.

06
Multi-Purpose Light

IPL — Intense Pulsed Light

Intense Pulsed Light (IPL) is a broad-spectrum, non-coherent light source that emits multiple wavelengths simultaneously — distinguishing it from single-wavelength lasers. By applying different cut-off filters, our specialists can selectively target specific chromophores: melanin (for pigmentation and hair removal), oxyhaemoglobin (for redness and vascular lesions), and P. acnes bacteria (for acne treatment). IPL's broad-spectrum output makes it one of the most versatile light-based platforms in aesthetic medicine — effectively treating hair removal, photorejuvenation, redness and flushing, acne, and general skin brightening within the same technology platform. Our IPL systems include both face and large-area body handpieces, enabling efficient treatment across all body zones.

07
Fat Reduction

Cryolipolysis

Cryolipolysis — commonly known as CoolSculpting in its branded form — is an FDA-cleared, non-surgical fat reduction technology based on the principle that fat cells are more susceptible to controlled cold temperatures than the overlying skin and other tissues. By applying a precisely controlled cooling applicator to targeted fat deposits, the technology induces cryoapoptosis — a natural cell death process — in the subcutaneous fat layer while leaving skin, nerves, and muscle entirely unaffected. The treated fat cells are gradually cleared by the lymphatic system over 6–12 weeks, resulting in a measurable reduction in fat layer thickness — typically 20–25% per treatment session. No needles, no surgery, no downtime.

08
Skin Healing & Rejuvenation

LED Light Therapy

LED (Light Emitting Diode) therapy delivers specific wavelengths of visible and near-infrared light that interact with cellular chromophores to produce distinct therapeutic effects without any thermal damage. Red light (630–660 nm) stimulates fibroblast activity and collagen production — improving skin tone, reducing fine lines, and accelerating post-treatment healing. Blue light (415–430 nm) targets and destroys P. acnes bacteria within follicles — the primary driver of acne — making it highly effective as both a standalone acne treatment and as an enhancer of other acne technologies. Near-infrared light (810–850 nm) penetrates deeply to reduce inflammation, accelerate tissue repair, and improve cellular energy metabolism. LED panels are used standalone and in combination with other treatments to enhance outcomes and comfort.

The Difference

Why Medical Grade Matters

The aesthetic device market is flooded with consumer-grade and entry-level clinic equipment that looks similar to medical-grade platforms but performs in an entirely different category. Understanding this difference is important when choosing where to invest in your skin.

  • Energy output: Medical-grade devices operate at significantly higher energy densities than consumer or entry-level equivalents — the therapeutic "dose" delivered to the target tissue is fundamentally different. Lower energy means less clinical effect, requiring more sessions for inferior results.
  • Wavelength precision: Genuine medical platforms deliver precisely calibrated wavelengths with tight spectral control. Consumer devices often produce broader, less precise output — less effective at selectively targeting the intended chromophore (melanin, bacteria, or fat).
  • Safety systems: Medical-grade devices include sophisticated real-time skin monitoring, cooling systems, and automatic parameter safeguards. These protect against adverse events and allow safe treatment across the full range of skin tones — particularly darker Asian skin.
  • Clinical validation: Medical-grade platforms have undergone rigorous clinical trials and regulatory review. The efficacy claims are backed by published data. Consumer and entry-level devices typically rely on marketing rather than clinical evidence.
  • Consistency: Medical-grade platforms are calibrated, serviced, and quality-controlled to deliver reproducible, consistent results from session to session and clinic to clinic. Consumer devices degrade quickly and produce inconsistent output.
  • Long-term investment: The higher cost of medical-grade treatment reflects the real cost of safe, effective technology — but also the real value of results that are genuinely worth having.
