About the Treatment

A Non-Surgical Facelift — Real Lifting, Real Results

Skin laxity — the gradual loosening of skin and the underlying facial tissues — is one of the most common and most visible aspects of facial aging. Sagging cheeks, jowl formation along the jawline, loose skin under the chin, drooping brow position, and neck laxity all contribute to a tired, aged appearance that cosmetics and skincare cannot reverse.

EstheClinic's skin tightening treatments use two of the most clinically advanced non-surgical technologies available: HIFU (High Intensity Focused Ultrasound) and Monopolar RF (Radiofrequency).

HIFU delivers focused ultrasound energy to the SMAS (Superficial Musculoaponeurotic System) layer — the deep facial tissue layer that surgeons physically tighten during a facelift. By reaching this depth non-invasively, HIFU triggers a structural lifting response that is genuinely comparable to early-stage surgical results — without any incisions.

Monopolar RF delivers deep, uniform heating throughout the dermis and subdermis, stimulating collagen contraction and new collagen production for progressive skin tightening over time. The two technologies work at different depths and through different mechanisms — making their combination exceptionally effective.

  • Sagging cheeks and mid-face laxity
  • Jowling and undefined jawline
  • Loose skin under the chin (submental area)
  • Drooping brow and upper eyelid heaviness
  • Neck laxity and banding

The Process

How It Works

Precision lifting from within — a structured journey to tighter, more defined skin.

  1. 01

    Facial Assessment

    Your consultation begins with a detailed facial assessment — our specialist evaluates the degree of skin laxity in each facial zone, the depth of the underlying tissue layers, and your overall skin health. We use advanced imaging to visualise the tissue structure and discuss the degree of lifting and tightening that is realistically achievable for your skin type and age. Your concerns, lifestyle, and expectations are central to the conversation.

  2. 02

    Mapping Treatment Areas

    Before each HIFU session, we conduct a precise anatomical mapping of your face. This is critical — HIFU energy is delivered to exact depths (1.5 mm, 3 mm, and 4.5 mm) and specific facial zones. Our specialists carefully map the treatment grid to maximise effectiveness while carefully avoiding sensitive structures. The mapping is reviewed and updated at each visit based on your progress.

  3. 03

    HIFU & RF Sessions

    The treatment session typically lasts 60–90 minutes for a full face and neck treatment. HIFU is delivered first — the transducer is placed against the skin and delivers focused lines of ultrasound energy in rapid pulses. You'll feel intermittent sensations of deep heat at the target depth. RF treatment follows, delivering a warm, comfortable tightening sensation across the skin. Most clients find the overall experience very manageable, especially with our topical preparation protocol.

  4. 04

    Collagen Synthesis Period

    After your treatment, the collagen rebuilding process begins. Over the following 8–12 weeks, new collagen fibres form and the tissue gradually tightens and lifts. We photograph and compare your results at 1-month and 3-month post-treatment visits. Most clients see their best results at the 3-month mark. For optimal lifting, a series of 2–3 sessions scheduled 4–6 weeks apart is typically recommended.

Your Questions Answered

Frequently Asked Questions

  • HIFU stands for High Intensity Focused Ultrasound. It works similarly to how a magnifying glass focuses sunlight to a single point — except it focuses ultrasound energy to precise depths within the facial tissue. At these focal points, the temperature rises to 60–70°C instantaneously, causing controlled thermal coagulation. This triggers an immediate collagen contraction (resulting in an instant tightening effect) and stimulates the body's wound-healing response — producing fresh collagen over the following weeks and months. HIFU is unique in being able to target the SMAS layer (4.5 mm depth) — the same structural layer addressed in surgical facelifts — entirely non-invasively.

  • HIFU results typically last 12–24 months, with most clients seeing results from 12–18 months before a maintenance session is beneficial. RF results last approximately 12–18 months. The longevity of results depends on your age, the degree of laxity treated, your lifestyle (sun protection, smoking, hydration), and how consistently you maintain your treatment schedule. Annual maintenance sessions are recommended to sustain and progressively improve results as the skin continues to age naturally.

  • HIFU is generally well-tolerated. The sensation varies by area and individual sensitivity — most clients describe it as intermittent pulses of deep heat or tingling, rating discomfort at around 4–6 out of 10 in bony areas (like the cheekbones and brow) and milder in fleshy areas (like the cheeks and neck). We apply a topical numbing cream 30 minutes before HIFU for sensitive clients. Importantly, any discomfort is brief and occurs only during the energy delivery — there is no lingering pain afterward.

  • For mild to moderate laxity, a single HIFU session combined with a series of 3–4 RF sessions typically produces excellent results. For more advanced laxity, 2–3 HIFU sessions may be recommended — spaced 4–6 weeks apart — with RF treatments in between to maintain and enhance the collagen-building effect. Your specialist will build a realistic treatment schedule based on your assessment results and aesthetic goals. We always aim for the most efficient path to your desired outcome.
